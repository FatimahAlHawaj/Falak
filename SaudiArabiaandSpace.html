Saudi Arabia and Space
المملكة العربية السعودية والفضاء

Prince Sultan bin Salman bin AbdulAziz Al Saud ( born 27 June 1956) is a former Royal saudi Airforce pilot who flew abroad the American STS-51-G Space Shuttle mission as a payload specialist, and a member of the house of Saud. He is the first member of a royal family to be an astronaut, and the first arab and muslim to fly in space. On 27 December 2018, he was appointed as chairnman of the board of Directors od the saudi Space Agency at the rank of minister.
STS-51-G was the 18th flight of NASA's Space Shuttle program, and the fifth flight of the Discovery Space Shuttle. The seven-day mission launched from Kennedy Space Center, Florida, on June 17, 1985, and landed at Edwards Air Force Base, California, on June 24.
